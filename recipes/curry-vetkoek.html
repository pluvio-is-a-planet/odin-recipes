Curry Vetkoek

Description

Vetkoek is a traditional South African fried dough bread. The vetkoek forms a part
of South African culture. It is similar to the Caribbean Johnny cake, the Dutch oliebol, and the
Mexican sopaipillas. It is also known by the Xhosa and Zulu name igwinya.
The word vetkoek literally means "fat cake" in Afrikaans.

Ingredients

Ground Beef Curry

  • 15ml (1 tbsp) olive oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1kg Ground Beef or Mince
  • 5ml (1 tbsp) curry paste
  • 15ml (1 tbsp) mild curry powder
  • 125ml (1/2 cup) apricot chutney
  • 60ml (1/4 cup) chilli jam
  • 60ml – 125ml (1/4 cup – ½ cup) water
  • 250ml (1 cup) mixed frozen vegetables
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Vetkoek

  • 1kg store-bought bread dough
  • 125ml (1/2 cup) flour
  • Cooking oil, for deep-frying

Steps

Ground Beef Curry

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Sauté the onions for about 5 minutes or until translucent,
    then add the garlic and sauté for another 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the ground beef and brown while stirring for about 10 minutes.
  4. Add the curry paste and curry powder to the ground beef mixture and fry for another 5 minutes.
  5. Add the chutney and chilli jam to the mixture and cook for another 15 minutes.
  6. Add the water if your mixture cooks too dry.
  7. Reduce the heat to a lower temperature and cook for another 15 minutes.
  8. Add the vegetable mix and cook for another 5 minutes. Season to taste.

Vetkoek

  1. Form the bread dough into balls just smaller than your fist and place it on
    a tray sprinkled with flour for it not to stick to the surface.
  2. Allow to rise for about 20 minutes in a warm place.
  3. Heat the oil in a large pot and fry each vetkoek for about 4-5 minutes per side or until golden brown and fully cooked.
